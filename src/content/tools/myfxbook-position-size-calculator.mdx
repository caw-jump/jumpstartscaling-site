## Position Sizing Math

To properly size a forex trade and manage drawdown, use:

`Lot Size = (Account Balance * Risk %) / (Stop Loss in Pips * Pip Value per Standard Lot)`

### How it's used
You input your total checking or margin account equity alongside your strict risk percentage per trade (e.g., 1%). Once you've analyzed the chart and located your Stop Loss distance, you input those pips and the current dollar-value of exactly 1 pip for that specific currency pair.

### Why it matters
Pip values change constantly based on live exchange rates. A 50-pip stop loss on EUR/USD carries a completely different financial risk than a 50-pip stop loss on GBP/JPY. 

This straightforward math prevents you from blowing up your account by ensuring 1% risk is precisely 1%, regardless of cross-currency conversions. Every professional uses a formula like this before issuing single order.
